The problem

Legacy monitoring leaves operators flying blind

Most wastewater facilities still rely on twice-daily grab sampling, single-parameter probes, and disconnected SCADA systems. Events are discovered after discharge. Process faults go unseen for months. Compliance depends on luck as much as process control.

The gap: No existing solution delivers continuous detection, actionable insight, and scalable coverage at the same time. AquaMesh is built specifically to close that gap.

Use cases

Where AquaMesh delivers value

Compliance
Effluent Discharge & NPDES Permit Monitoring
AquaSpectra probes at secondary clarifier effluent and final outfall provide continuous TOC, nitrate, turbidity, and UV254 readings. AquaView's AI forecasts parameter trajectories and alerts operators 45–90 minutes before a threshold breach — enough time to adjust aeration, dosing, or flow. Automated DMR-compatible summaries replace manual reporting prep.
↓ 87% permit exceedance events in year one
Process QA
Upstream Process Fault Detection
Sensors across the process train — influent, primary clarifiers, aeration basins — feed AquaView alongside existing SCADA data. Correlation analysis identifies the root cause of effluent quality changes upstream, before they reach the outfall. Slow-developing faults like polymer line leaks or gradual aeration degradation surface weeks earlier than grab sampling would reveal.
Reuse & Storage
Reclaimed Water & Pond Monitoring
Continuous chlorophyll-a, phycocyanin, and fDOM monitoring at storage ponds detects algal bloom precursors before they affect distribution. AquaLink hubs with Starlink D2C connectivity remove the need for new conduit or cellular infrastructure at remote sites.
Early bloom detection at 3 sites previously unmonitored
Stormwater
Spill & CSO Detection
Rapid-deploy mesh nodes across drainage channels and containment areas come online within hours. AquaSpectra's oil/hydrocarbon fluorescence channel catches spill signatures in real time. AquaView integrates weather forecasts to auto-tighten alert thresholds ahead of rainfall events.
Multi-site
Utility Network Management
AquaLink gateways at each site — pump stations, lift stations, satellite plants — feed into one AquaView dashboard with consistent alert protocols. Cross-site benchmarking surfaces underperforming facilities. OTA updates reach all nodes simultaneously with no site visits.

Deployment

Live in days, not weeks

A typical 10–15 node network deploys in two to three days — no conduit, no licensed electrician, no cellular SIM per node.

1
Site assessment & monitoring point mapping
Map monitoring locations across your process train — influent, clarifiers, aeration basins, UV disinfection, final outfall, and any remote ponds or pump stations.
2
AquaLink mesh deployment
Hubs mount on existing poles or new posts. Nearby nodes self-discover and form a mesh network automatically — typically fully configured in under two hours. One gateway uplinks to your facility network or Starlink D2C.
3
AquaSpectra probe installation
Probes connect to AquaLink hubs via 4-wire RS-485 cable. Third-party sensors (pH, DO, TSS, flow) plug into the same hub inputs with no additional hardware.
4
AquaView setup & integration
Permit thresholds, alert preferences, and SCADA/LIMS integrations are configured. Live dashboards are operational within 24 hours of hardware deployment.
5
Commissioning & handoff
AquaMesh delivers a commissioning report, a remote training walkthrough, and ongoing support via the AquaView SaaS subscription. All future firmware updates deploy over the air — no return visits needed.
